About Walking In The Sun Rescue

Walking In The Sun Rescue is a 501(c)(3) organisation— rescuing dogs for years but becoming “official” in 2014.

Our mission is to promote spay and neuter, find loving indoor homes for the homeless, treat the ill and injured, comfort those who are suffering, and educate the community.

We operate without a brick-and-mortar shelter; every life is saved through a network of foster homes. We take in animals when space and finances allow, especially those that would not be accepted by the local shelter—or to keep them from entering it in the first place.

All pets are fully vetted in foster care, then placed in permanent homes or transported to partner rescues across the United States.

Because of generous donors and fund-raisers, our dogs leave a world of darkness and learn to walk in the sun every day.

"Saving one dog will not change the world, but surely for that one dog, the world will change forever."

Our 2024 Impact

  • 369 animals saved
  • 594 animals transported
  • 50 animals spayed & neutered

Our 2025 Goals

  • Save 500 dogs through rescue or transport.
  • Increase Low-Cost Spay & Neuter Assistance by 50 % (≈30 more pets per year).
  • Increase Community Outreach by engaging with the public in more local events

Why Our Parish Needs Us

Morehouse Parish’s median household income (\$32,929) and 31 % poverty rate mean routine veterinary care is out of reach for many families. Only one brick-and-mortar veterinary clinic operates in the parish, so our mobile, volunteer-driven program fills a critical gap in services.

Community Partners

We rely on the generosity of North-east LA veterinarians Dr. Chris Morris (Rayville), Dr. Tyler Thomas (West Monroe) and Dr. Jennifer Ratliff (Ruston), plus outreach hosts such as PetSmart (Monroe) and Tractor Supply (West Monroe & Bastrop).
